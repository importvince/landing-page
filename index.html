You can learn to code with a deformity.

Some people are born with physical differences, such as missing fingers, toes, arms, legs, and more. This doesn't mean they can't code.

Some famous D4MD coders

  • Farida Bedwei has cerebral palsy. Despite her condition, she learned to program and ultimately developed a cloud software used by 100+ companies in Ghana.
  • Albert Enstein had a learning disability, similar to dislexia. Despite this, he was able to help humanity make giant leaps in our understanding of our universe, through mathematics and physics.
  • Stephen Hawking was disgnosed with amyotrophic lateral scleoris at age 21. Using creative ways to communicate despite his condition, Stephen contributed groundbreaking theories about the origins of the universe, black holes, and radation.
  • Many don't know that Thomas Edison lost much of his hearing in his early 20s. Despite this setback, Thomas thrived as an inventor and innovator in the field of telegraphy. He most famously invented the lightbulb.
