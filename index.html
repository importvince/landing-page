You can do anything you set your mind to.

Some people are born with physical disabilities, such as missing ligaments, missing senses, and muscular disorders. Some are born with mental disabilities, such as autism or learning disorders. While these can be signficant challenges, they do not prevent learning and achievement.

Successful people with disabilities


  • Farida Bedwei has cerebral palsy. Despite her condition, she learned to program and ultimately developed a cloud software used by 100+ companies in Ghana.

  • Albert Enstein had a learning disability, similar to dislexia. Despite this, he was able to help humanity make giant leaps in our understanding of our universe, through mathematics and physics.

  • Using creative ways to communicate despite having amyotrophic lateral scleoris, Stephen Hawking contributed groundbreaking theories about the origins of the universe, black holes, and radation.

  • Thomas Edison lost much of his hearing in his early 20s. Despite this setback, Thomas thrived as an inventor and innovator in the field of telegraphy. He most famously invented the lightbulb.

"There is no greater disability in society, than the inability to see a person as more."

- Robert M. Hensel

Go be great!
